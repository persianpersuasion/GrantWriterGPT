# GrantWriterGPT

## Project Overview

The Community Grant Writer is an AI-powered grant development platform designed specifically for nonprofit organizations in San Diego County. The platform leverages large language models (LLMs), historical grant application data, and structured organizational information to assist community organizations in preparing competitive grant applications for selected local funding opportunities.

Unlike general-purpose AI writing tools, the Community Grant Writer is purpose-built and trained around specific San Diego funding programs, enabling organizations to generate tailored, high-quality draft responses that align with the priorities, language, and evaluation criteria of local grantmakers.

The initial platform will support a targeted set of high-impact regional funding opportunities, including:

* County of San Diego Community Enhancement Program (CEP)
* San Diego Foundation grant opportunities
* City of San Diego Community Projects, Programs, and Services (CPPS) Grant

Future versions may expand to additional municipal, county, foundation, and state funding programs.

## Problem Statement

Many community-based organizations operate with limited administrative capacity and often lack dedicated grant-writing staff. As a result, organizations may:

* Miss funding opportunities due to time constraints.
* Submit incomplete or inconsistent applications.
* Struggle to articulate program impact effectively.
* Incur significant costs hiring external grant consultants.

These barriers disproportionately affect smaller nonprofits and emerging community organizations.

## Project Objectives

The Community Grant Writer seeks to:

1. Reduce the time required to prepare grant applications.
2. Increase the quality and consistency of grant narratives.
3. Improve funding accessibility for small and midsized nonprofit organizations.
4. Capture and replicate characteristics commonly found in successful grant applications.
5. Provide an affordable alternative to traditional grant-writing services.

## Core Functionality

The platform will assist users through the following workflow:

### Organizational Profile Creation

Organizations complete a one-time intake questionnaire containing:

* Mission and vision statements
* Organizational history
* Target populations served
* Program descriptions
* Geographic service areas
* Budget and financial information
* Existing partnerships and outcomes data

This information becomes a reusable organizational knowledge base.

### Automated Application Population

For supported grant programs, the platform will:

* Automatically populate repetitive application fields.
* Recommend responses based on previously entered organizational information.
* Reuse approved content across multiple applications.
* Generate complete first-draft narratives aligned with grant requirements.

### AI-Assisted Narrative Development

Using a combination of grant guidelines, historical applications, and prompt engineering, the system will:

* Draft responses to narrative questions.
* Recommend language emphasizing measurable outcomes and community impact.
* Align responses with funder priorities and scoring criteria.
* Suggest supporting metrics and evidence where appropriate.

### Review and Export

Users may edit, approve, and export generated applications for final submission.

Human review and organizational approval will remain required prior to submission.

## Technical Scope

Potential project tasks include:

* Collecting and structuring historical grant applications.
* Extracting and labeling successful narrative patterns.
* Building retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) pipelines.
* Developing prompt templates and evaluation frameworks.
* Fine-tuning large language model workflows.
* Building a secure web interface for nonprofit users.
* Creating automated document generation and export capabilities.
* Establishing quality assurance and hallucination detection processes.

## Expected Deliverables

* Web-based grant-writing platform prototype.
* Organizational intake and profile management system.
* AI narrative generation engine.
* Support for 2–3 San Diego grant programs.
* User dashboard and application export functionality.
* Pilot implementation with selected nonprofit organizations.

## Target Users

* Small and midsized nonprofit organizations
* Community-based organizations (CBOs)
* Grassroots organizations
* Volunteer-led nonprofits
* Organizations without dedicated grant-writing staff

## Long-Term Vision

The Community Grant Writer will serve as a scalable, subscription-based platform that democratizes access to grant funding by enabling organizations of all sizes to produce stronger, more competitive applications while reducing administrative burden.

By lowering barriers to funding access, the platform aims to strengthen the nonprofit ecosystem across San Diego County and increase the capacity of organizations serving local communities.
